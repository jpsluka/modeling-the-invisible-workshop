Competition Rules

The workshop is a forecasting competition focused on influenza-associated hospitalizations.

The competition is organized as two independent challenges (two years), each with three sequential rounds.

Team structure

  • Teams may contain between 1 and 4 participants.
  • Each participant may belong to only one team.
  • Teams may use any software, programming language, modeling framework, or workflow.

Forecast targets

  • weekly influenza-associated hospitalizations per 100,000 population
  • effective reproduction number (r0)

Time system

  • 40-week influenza season
  • integer week numbers only
  • Week 1 is the season start
  • No calendar dates in submission files

Submission rules

  • One CSV submission per challenge round
  • Full predicted weekly trajectory from Week 1 through the release-defined forecast horizon
  • Submissions are automatically validated and scored

Allowed methods

  • mechanistic epidemic models
  • statistical forecasting models
  • machine learning methods
  • Bayesian approaches
  • agent-based simulations
  • hybrid or ensemble methods
  • external public data sources
  • publicly available software libraries

Only explicit prohibition

The only activity explicitly forbidden is hacking into the organizer's or other workshop participants' computer systems.
